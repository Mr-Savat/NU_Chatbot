Q: What is Norton University?
A: Norton University (NU) is one of the oldest, largest, and most well-established private universities in Cambodia. It was founded in 1996 and is known for its strong academic programs, particularly in Business, IT, and Law.

Q: Where is Norton University located?
A: The main campus is located in Phnom Penh, the capital city of Cambodia. The address is: St. Keo Chenda, Sangkat Chroy Changvar, Khan Chroy Changvar, Phnom Penh.

Q: Is Norton University accredited?
A: Yes, Norton University is fully accredited by the Royal Government of Cambodia's Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport (MoEYS). It is recognized as a leading private higher education institution in the country.

Q: What is the vision of Norton University?
A: Norton University's vision is to be a premier university in Cambodia and the region, dedicated to providing high-quality education, fostering research, and developing future leaders who can contribute to societal development.

Q: What is the mission of Norton University?
A: Its mission is to provide accessible, high-quality education through effective teaching, research, and community service, preparing graduates with the knowledge, skills, and ethical values needed for success in a globalized world.

Q: What programs does Norton University offer?
A: NU offers a wide range of programs at the Associate's, Bachelor's, Master's, and Doctoral levels. Key faculties include: Business and Economics, Law, Science and Technology, Arts Humanities and Languages, and Social Sciences.

Q: Does Norton University teach in English?
A: Yes, the primary language of instruction for most degree programs, especially at the undergraduate and graduate levels, is English. This prepares students for the international job market.

Q: Does Norton University offer IT or Computer Science programs?
A: Yes, the Faculty of Science and Technology offers popular programs like Computer Science, Information Technology, and Software Engineering.

Q: Does Norton University have a good Law program?
A: Yes, the Faculty of Law is one of the most renowned and popular faculties at Norton University, known for its rigorous curriculum and experienced lecturers.

Q: Can I get an MBA at Norton University?
A: Yes, the Faculty of Business and Economics offers a Master of Business Administration (MBA) program, among other graduate business degrees.

Q: How can I apply to Norton University?
A: You can apply directly through the Norton University website or by visiting the Admissions Office on campus. The process typically involves submitting an application form, required documents, and paying an application fee.

Q: What are the entry requirements for a Bachelor's degree?
A: Generally, applicants need a high school diploma (or equivalent) and a satisfactory grade. Specific requirements may vary by faculty. It's best to check the official university website for the most accurate details.

Q: What documents are needed for application?
A: Commonly required documents include: 1. High School Certificate 2. Transcript of Records 3. Birth Certificate 4. ID Card or Passport Photos 5. Application Form

Q: Does Norton University require an entrance exam?
A: Some faculties may require an entrance exam or an interview as part of the admissions process. Please contact the admissions office for program-specific requirements.

Q: When is the application deadline?
A: Norton University typically has intakes for new students at the start of each semester. Specific deadlines can change, so you should check their official academic calendar or contact admissions for the exact dates.

Q: How much is tuition at Norton University?
A: Tuition fees vary significantly depending on the faculty and program (e.g., Law may have a different fee structure than IT). It is best to contact the university's finance department directly for the most current and precise fee schedule. As a private university, fees are typically charged per credit.

Q: Are there scholarships available?
A: Yes, Norton University offers various scholarships and financial aid options based on academic merit, talent, and financial need. Details on available scholarships and application procedures can be found on their website.

Q: Can I pay tuition in installments?
A: Yes, the university generally offers flexible payment plans, allowing students to pay tuition fees on a semester-by-semester or monthly basis. Confirm the specific options with the finance office.

Q: Does Norton University have a library?
A: Yes, NU has a modern, well-equipped library with a vast collection of books, journals, and digital resources to support student learning and research.

Q: Does the university have computer labs?
A: Yes, there are multiple computer labs with internet access available for students to use for their studies and research.

Q: Is there student housing or a dormitory?
A: Norton University does not typically have on-campus dormitories. However, the admissions office can often provide guidance or information on finding accommodation near the campus in Phnom Penh.

Q: Are there clubs and student activities?
A: Yes, student life is active with various clubs, associations, and organizations covering academic, cultural, sports, and social interests. There are also regular seminars, workshops, and guest lectures.

A: The university has a Career Center that provides services like career counseling, internship opportunities, resume writing workshops, and job placement assistance to help graduates transition into the workforce.
